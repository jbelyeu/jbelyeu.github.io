Interests and some history

I'm still formulating my career direction, since recently increased contact with the research world has shown me so many attractive options. I've had opportunities to work in three different research labs while an undergraduate student at BYU (with Dr. John Prince in Biochemistry, and with Dr. Perry Ridge and Dr. Rick Gill in Biology), which gave me an interest in research. I've also been able to attend a research conference (BioT, where I presented a poster that I'll discuss below), visit and present at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, and visit several graduate schools for interviews.

I've decided based on all of this that I'll be happiest researching in genetics, with a heavy focus on computational tools. That's the direction I intend to take in grad school at the University of Utah, although I'm open to changes of plan as I get involved with the program.

Codon Bias

I worked with Justin Miller, under the supervision of Dr. Ridge, in a project targetted toward understanding evolutionary implications of non-random codon bias. A paper titled "USING CODON NON-USAGE IN PHYLOGENIES" is under review for publication.

SelecT

I worked with Hayden Smith, again supervised by Dr. Ridge, on a project to implement and improve the Composite of Multiple Signals study as a Java program. Our work, now managed by Brandon Pickett, is named SelecT (Selection Testing) and we plan submission for publication by the end of this summer. Preliminary results were presented in a poster session at BYU.

Seismic Event Detector

In a project for a BYU CS Capstone in Big Data, my team (Artem Golotin, Darian Ramage, and I) developed a scalable system for monitoring seismic signals in a developing aftershock sequence. This could be used to study earthquakes in more detail, as well as to identify illegal nuclear testing events from their aftershock signature. This was presented in person at a special meeting held at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory and in a poster session with short talks at BYU.
